#### The science (hypothesis & motivation)

**Why it died (the lesson, first):** the classifier really does predict something — the label-permutation null PASSES at z = +6.2 (`audit.md:36`) — but what it predicts is **answer length**, not hallucination. Hallucinated answers are systematically longer (median 142 vs 38 tokens, Mann-Whitney p = 9.4e-5, `audit.md:43`); the wavelet DC/low-frequency band energy tracks length (Spearman ρ = 0.79, `audit.md:44`); and once length is regressed out, balanced accuracy collapses to 0.53, CI [0.49, 0.57] ≈ chance (`audit.md:45-46`). Probes 2–4 of the confound audit all FAIL (`audit.md:67-71`), the cumulative-AND gate returns `Survives: NO` (`audit.md:86`), and H-001's own falsification criterion is met (`hypothesis.md:17-21`). The independent results-verifier issues REJECT — not BLOCKED, because the metric WAS logged and is scorable (`verdict.md:21`) — and the experiment is retracted (`trace/EXP-spectral-length-bias.md:25`).

**Fixture framing (honest):** this whole experiment is a synthetic acceptance fixture (`fixture: true`, `experiment.json:40`; `approved: "fixture — no human sign-off"`, `experiment.json:39`). It re-enacts the proteus Eksperyment-1 self-retraction to prove the harness's confound-audit gate works (`README.md:1-6`). The numbers are the fixture's canonical numbers, consistent across the bundle/stats/audit, but no real GPU run is reproducible from this repo. It is excluded from real findings (`atlas.yaml:21`).

**The full H-001 card** (`hypothesis.md:8-49`):

- **Statement:** "Wavelet-band energy of H-Neuron activation trajectories detects hallucination" (`hypothesis.md:8`).
- **Prediction (EARS):** "When an L2-regularized logistic classifier on the Daubechies-4 (3-level) wavelet-band energy features of H-Neuron activation trajectories is evaluated on the qid-disjoint held-out fold (keyed by question id, the unit of scientific interest), it shall achieve balanced_accuracy >= 0.65 (chance = 0.50)." - **Kill condition:** "balanced_accuracy on the qid-disjoint held-out fold <= chance + margin (i.e. <= 0.55), OR the positive signal is explained by a confound (the confound-audit returns `Survives: NO`). Either observation REJECTS H-001." (`hypothesis.md:17-21`). The second leg is the one that fired.
- **Named confounds (pre-registered, each mapping to one RULE-6 audit leg):** label-permutation null (K=1000); **answer length as the pre-named PRIMARY confound** (DC-band energy ≈ mean signal magnitude ≈ length; fixed-32 resampling entangles them further); statistic-swap to length-invariant wavelet scattering; alt-preprocessing dropping the fixed-32 resampling (`hypothesis.md:22-37`).
- **Motivation/novelty:** a cheap spectral hallucination detector from internal H-Neuron dynamics, which must beat length/perplexity heuristics — "the open question is precisely whether a spectral feature adds signal BEYOND answer length" (`hypothesis.md:43-47`). The answer turned out to be: it does not.

#### How it works — mechanics

There is **no run code in this repo** — the runs executed on a remote host whose `host-config.json` is gitignored and absent from the fixture (`experiment.json:30`; `design.md:100-102`). The walk below follows the method as frozen in `prereg.lock:13-26` and `design.md` Methods, with the `runs/RUN-001/` bundle as the evidence of what actually ran; off-repo symbols are those cited by `model-info-sheet.md` (unverifiable here — fixture).

2. **Trajectory extraction.** For each H-Neuron, its activation trajectory `x[1..T]` across the tokens of an answer is the raw signal (`design.md:42-44`); activations come from frozen models that never saw the labels (`model-info-sheet.md` L2.5).
3. **Fixed-length resampling.** Each variable-length trajectory is resampled to length 32 — `trajectory_resample_length: 32` (`config.resolved.json:6`). This is D-001, the choice that later manufactures the artifact.
4. **Spectral feature.** Daubechies-4 DWT, 3 levels → 4 wavelet-band energies per (sample, layer, neuron): one approximation/DC band + three detail bands, `bands: ["A3","D3","D2","D1"]` (`config.resolved.json:7-9`; `design.md:44-47`). Features concatenate across the screened H-Neurons and layers, then per-model z-score (`config.resolved.json:10`; RULE-4).
5. **Leakage-safe fitting.** Inside the qid-grouped CV loop, the StandardScaler, the DWT-energy normalizer, the H-Neuron screen and the logistic C-search are fit on train folds ONLY — off-repo `src/featurize.py:make_pipeline` and `src/cv.py:nested_cv` per `model-info-sheet.md` L1.2-L1.3 (unverifiable in-repo — fixture); `fit_scope: "train_fold_only_inside_cv"` (`config.resolved.json:16`).
6. **Classifier.** L2-regularized logistic regression, resolved `C: 0.5` (`config.resolved.json:12-15`), predicting HALLUCINATED vs FAITHFUL.
7. **Held-out scored ONCE.** The qid-disjoint held-out fold is read exactly once for the headline metric (`design.md:52-53`; `model-info-sheet.md` L1.7) → `held_out_balanced_accuracy: 0.71` (`metrics.json:6`).

**Algorithms & formulas:**

- **Spectral statistic** (verbatim from the hashed plan): "Per H-Neuron, resample its activation trajectory x[1..T] over an answer's tokens to fixed length 32, then take a Daubechies-4 DWT (3 levels) yielding 4 wavelet-band energies per (sample, layer, neuron)" (`prereg.lock:14-16`). - **Primary metric:** `balanced_accuracy` on the qid-disjoint held-out fold, chance baseline 0.50 (`prereg.lock:11,18-19`).
- **CI method:** bootstrap BCa over qids, n_resamples = 2000, variation source = qid-split (`prereg.lock:19-20`; `config.resolved.json:28`). Effect size = balanced-accuracy delta over chance (RULE-7, `prereg.lock:20`).

- **Permutation scheme** (verbatim from the audit): labels "shuffled WITHIN the qid-disjoint fold, never across the test boundary" (`audit.md:26`), K=1000; `z = (0.71 − 0.500) / 0.034 = +6.2`; `p_perm = (#null >= true + 1)/(K + 1) = 1/1001 ≈ 1.0e-3` (`audit.md:33-34`).

#### Design decisions (DDRs, with trade-offs)

- **D-001 — Fixed-length-32 resampling of variable-length trajectories** (`design.md:64-75`). *Decision:* resample every trajectory of variable token length T to a fixed length of 32 before the DWT, to put all answers on a common spectral basis. *Alternative considered:* a length-invariant wavelet-scattering transform (no resampling). *Trade-off accepted:* simplicity and a cheap DWT, while explicitly noting that "resampling to a constant length can entangle the DC/low-frequency band energy with the original answer length, which is the exact threat the confound-audit must screen." *Flip trigger:* adopting the scattering transform as primary changes the primary feature definition and forces a `/register` round-trip (RULE-5). *Outcome:* the noted trade-off is precisely what killed the experiment — the audit's probes 3 and 4 deliberately mirror this DDR, and both FAILED. This was the only DDR (`design.md:147`) and it was load-bearing.

#### Gotchas & lessons

This section is the deliverable — the fixture exists to teach these:

1. **A permutation-null PASS is necessary, never sufficient.** z = +6.2 proves the classifier predicts *something* beyond random labels — it says nothing about *what*. Probe 2 answers that, and the answer was "length" (`audit.md:37`; `verdict.md:18`; `atlas.yaml:15`). A pipeline that stops at the permutation test would have shipped a false detector with p_adj = 4e-6.
2. **Two gates, two failure classes.** `/data-audit` PASS and `/confound-audit` FAIL are not in tension: the qid-disjoint split is genuinely clean (0 straddles, train-fold-only reducers, held-out scored once) — a *confound* is a validity threat, not a leakage event (`data-audit.md:16`; `verdict.md:26`; `README.md:12-21`). Diagnosing this as "leakage" would have led to fixing the wrong thing.
3. **Freeze audit thresholds BEFORE computing probes.** The thresholds (z*=3.0, alpha*=0.05, t*=0.05, r*=80%, AND-gate) were written 2026-05-28T14:03:11Z, before the probe compute at 15:20:00Z — the dual timestamps in `audit.md:12` and `trace:17-18` are what let the verifier certify "the FAIL is honest, not engineered" (`verdict.md:25`). Without that freeze the retraction itself would be ungradable.
4. **Pre-name the nuisance variables at registration.** Answer length was named the primary confound in the H-001 card (`hypothesis.md:26-32`) and the run logged the raw length stats into `metrics.json:18-27` *before* any audit — so the kill was mechanical, not a judgement call. The length-only baseline "reaches the same ~0.71, which is the whole point" (`model-info-sheet.md` L2.7).
5. **The load-bearing DDR became the murder weapon.** D-001's fixed-32 resampling was flagged at design time as the step that could entangle DC-band energy with length; probes 3 and 4 deliberately mirror it, and dropping it retains only ~24% of the effect (`audit.md:58-61`). Mirror suspicious DDRs as audit legs.
6. **REJECT ≠ BLOCKED.** The metric was logged and citable, so the claim was scorable; it fails on the merits (`verdict.md:21`). BLOCKED is reserved for never-logged metrics (`README.md:50-54`).
7. **A retraction is a finding (RULE-8).** The dead end is recorded in the tree with its `why`, and the prescribed continuation is a *re-registration* with a length-controlled PRIMARY metric and a new prereg_hash — not a quiet re-run (`verdict.md:17-18`; `atlas.yaml:8`).
8. **Fixture bookkeeping quirks to know:** the RUN-001 directory serves double duty — T-001's quicklook wrote it first (0.69 thin slice, `runs.md:61`) and the bundled contents are T-004's headline sweep (`metrics.json:4`); RUN-002/RUN-003 exist only as Run Notes, their bundles are not in the repo; `runs.md`/`trace` use the path `results/EXP-spectral-length-bias/RUN-001/` while the bundle actually sits at `experiments/EXP-spectral-length-bias/runs/RUN-001/`; and all run code (`src/featurize.py`, `src/cv.py`, `tests/test_split.py`) plus `host-config.json` and the T-001 quicklook notebook are absent — off-repo by design (fixture).
